GPT-2 (Generative Pre-trained Transformer 2) is a large language model developed by OpenAI. It is designed to generate coherent and contextually relevant text based on input prompts. GPT-2 uses the Transformer architecture, which allows it to process and generate sequential text efficiently. Key characteristics of GPT-2 include: Unsupervised pretraining: Trained on a massive dataset of internet text without task-specific supervision. Large scale: It features up to 1.5 billion parameters (in its largest version). Text generation: Capable of producing human-like text, completing prompts, translating, summarizing, and answering questions. General-purpose: It works across a variety of NLP tasks without fine-tuning. OpenAI initially limited its release due to concerns about its potential misuse (e.g., generating fake news), but later made it publicly available in phases. GPT-2 marked a significant step toward large-scale language models like GPT-3 and GPT-4.
